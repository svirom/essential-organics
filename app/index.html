Energy Superfood #1:
Slow-Release “Power” Carbs

Carbohydrates are CRUCIAL for keeping your battery fully charged throughout the day.

It’s why almost ALL cultures around the world include carbs in their breakfasts. Whether that’s bread in Britain… Pastries in France… Or rice and noodles in Asia.

But not all carbs are created equal!

Let me explain…

If you want long-lasting energy - Your body needs carbs which release energy slowly. Because otherwise you get a short-term energy burst, followed by a savage slump.

AKA a sugar crash.

See, the way scientists measure how quickly carbs release energy is by something called the “Glycemic Index”...

Or GI for short.

For example, white bread has a sky-high GI of 100. Meaning your body burns through it in just a few hours.

Leaving you tired, sluggish, and craving MORE carbs.

But as I said, if you want a constant flow of all-day energy - you need slow-release carbs, which you can absorb steadily. Not all at once.

In other words:

Low GI carbs.

When it comes to slow-release energy…

The lower the Glycemic Index, the better. And some of the best slow-release carbs for all-day energy are:

Activated Barley

Harvested at its most nutritious… Activated barley has an astoundingly low GI index of 20. And also contains high levels of fibre, as well as immune-boosting beta-glucans.

Making it a powerful weapon in your fatigue-fighting arsenal. And a great way to naturally support good health.

Broccoli Sprout

Another key energy superfood - Broccoli Sprout has a GI 10x lower than white bread!

What’s more, broccoli sprouts contain 50x more health-boosting phytonutrients than normal broccoli…

Nutrients which reduce your risk of cancer, heart disease, stroke, Alzheimer's and Parkinson's.[8]

Wheat Grass

This super-sprout is taken from the shoots of wheat grains. But as it contains no sugar, it has a rock-bottom GI of under 20.

And here’s the most impressive part:

A study published in the “Journal of Herbal Medicine and Toxicology” looked at the amazing health benefits of Wheat Grass. And found not ONLY does it have a low GI…

It even lowers the GI of other carbs you eat![9]

So if you want long-lasting daily energy - without the mid-afternoon crash… Including these 3 low GI “power” carbs in your diet is a must.
